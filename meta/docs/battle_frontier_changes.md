# Facilities
Silicon's Battle Frontier will have the following facilities:
- Battle Tower
- Battle Arcade
- Restricted Sparring
- Battle Factory

# Battle Points
Instead of recieving Battle Points being rewarded after every challenge, Battle Points are rewarded after every battle. The number of awarded points changes based on the player's current winstreak.

# Challenge
The Frontier is no longer divded in challenges that are 7 battles long. The player's winstreak will continue to increment until the player loses. With the exception of Restricted Sparring, after any battle, the player can pause the current challenge and come back later to resume where they left off. Playes can freely change Pokemon upon leaving the facility.

Restricted Sparring can be paused by saving and exiting the game, but leaving the facility will reset the current win streak.

The Battle Factory will reset the player's party every 5 wins.

All formulas that rely on number of challenges will instead use the length of the winstreak to calculate.

# Boss Fights
Rather than boss battles occuring at the end of the 3th and 7th challenges, boss battles will occur at the end every battle that is a multiple of 50. Boss Battles could be any of the main characters of the game. Bosses are not locked into a specific team, but rather pull from a pool of Pokemon for that specific boss.

# Difficulty
All non-Boss trainers in the Battle Frontier pull from a single pool of all 756 (189 fully evolved Resido mons * 4 sets) Pokemon. This pool remains the same regardless of the player's win streak and does not increase over time.

# Restricted Pokemon
The only Special Pokemon in the game are the Silvally line, Urshifu line, and all the Ultra Beasts. The player is not allowed to use any of these Pokemon in the Battle Frontier, and the opponent will never use these Pokemon.

# Single / Double / Multis
Every facility has three modes - Singles, Doubles and Multis (with a link or NPC partner).

# Bring X Pick Y / Doubles
Before entering a facility, the player must bring a full party of six non-Egg Pokemon. Before every battle, the player will see the opponents party of six Pokemon. They will then choose x Pokemon to bring into the battle. X is 3 Pokemon for Single modes, 4 Pokemon for Double modes, and 2 Pokemon for Multi modes.
